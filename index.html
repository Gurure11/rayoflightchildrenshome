About Ray of Light

Ray of Light Children’s Centre is a registered Private Voluntary Organization founded in 2011 to provide holistic care and support to orphans and vulnerable children.

The organization has helped children gain access to education, nutritious food, clothing and emotional support. Some beneficiaries have gone on to tertiary education.

The centre now focuses on supporting child-headed families affected by poverty, migration, HIV/AIDS and the impact of COVID-19.

History of the Orphanage

Ray of Light Children’s Centre was founded in 2011 by Patricia Vutete and Constantino Chapeta, with the help of Catherine Robinson and her late husband John. The project was implemented under the guidance of Fr Nigel Johnson, whose oversight helped shape the centre’s early mission of care, dignity and hope.

Over the years, Ray of Light has supported orphans and vulnerable children by helping them access education, nutritious food, proper clothing and emotional support. The work has opened doors for many children, including some who later progressed to tertiary education.

Today, the centre continues to respond to the growing needs of child-headed families, giving children practical help and a pathway toward a safer, brighter future.
